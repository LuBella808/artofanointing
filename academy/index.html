Anointed Artist Academy

The Invitation

What if your creativity
was always a calling?

The Anointed Artist Academy exists at the intersection of ancient wisdom and living practice. It is formation, not just instruction. It is a transmission of what anointing, beauty, and embodied prayer can actually do in a life, and how to carry that into the lives of others.

This is for the creative woman who has felt the pull toward something sacred in her making. Who has sensed that her studio is a sanctuary, that her hands carry something beyond technique, that the act of creation can be a form of prayer.

She is simply remembering what she has always carried.

The Practice

What you carry out of this.

Embodied Theology

A grounded understanding of anointing through scripture, inspiration, and the wisdom of the senses. Not doctrine, but living practice, open to the mystic and the seeker alike.

Sacred Creativity

The Anointed Artist method: how to create from a place of prayer, how to anoint your work, how to hold space for others in their creative encounter with God.

A Way to Lead

A framework for guiding others through sacred creativity, anointing rituals, and embodied prayer in retreats, workshops, and your own practice.

"Heaven is already pressing through the visible world.
Beauty opens the door."

Two Paths of Formation

Choose your threshold.

The Studio

Personal
Formation

A cohort-based immersion in the Art of Anointing practice. Over the course of our time together, you will move through the theology, history, and embodied practice of anointing, learn the Anointed Artist method, and enter more deeply into your own creative prayer life.

For the creative woman who is ready to go deeper. Who senses that her art and her faith belong in the same room. Who wants to be formed, not just informed.

The Certification

Certified
Facilitator

For women called to guide others. The Anointed Artist Facilitator Certification prepares you to lead this work in your own practice, retreats, workshops, and community, with the methodology and confidence to hold this space well.

This is not simply a course. It is an ordination of sorts. A recognition that you are ready to carry something forward and pass it on.

Who This Is For

You have always known
your art was a prayer.

You are a creative; a visionary, a painter, a poet, a maker, who has felt the sacred pressing through your work, even when you couldn't name it.

Perhaps you are drawn to anointing, to ritual, to the idea that beauty and prayer are not separate things.

You are ready to deepen your own practice, or you are called to guide others into theirs.

You do not need to have it all figured out. You need only feel the pull.
